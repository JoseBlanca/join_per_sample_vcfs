## 1. Overall assessment

The design is internally elaborate and, on its *core new machinery*, mostly sound: the
empirical candidate assembly, the sum-over-slips likelihood with slip-placement
marginalized inside `align` (a faithful HipSTR borrow — verified), the integer-counted
pre-pass sufficient statistics, and the absorbing-trap guards (`π=0` floor, `F=0.99`
ceiling) all survive scrutiny. **The design is weakest precisely where the 2026-06-20/21
"chemistry per sample group" amendment bolted a *frozen, per-group* parameter axis onto a
model whose determinism, identifiability, and "low-stakes seed contamination" arguments
were all written for *refinable* parameters.** Freezing the stutter **level** and **ε**
silently re-opens the masquerading-het hazard the prior review's Issue 5 thought it had
closed (C2), and the per-sample/per-group cache story is internally contradictory and
breaks the project's headline memory thesis in its worst case (M2). Independently, the
**ε-vs-stutter "different coordinates" identifiability claim collapses at period 1**
(mononucleotides — the most common, most stutter-prone plant STR class), and the design is
actually *more* exposed there than HipSTR, which deliberately separates the two (C1).
Several determinism and "calls-don't-move" claims are asserted but not established (M1,
M4). The prior-art attributions added in the new parameters doc overreach what the cited
code supports (M3, M6).

Net: the *genotyping-on-supplied-parameters* spine (roadmap Milestone C) looks buildable
and largely correct. The *parameter pre-pass and the freeze decisions* (Milestone D) are
where the load-bearing risk concentrates and where I'd hold the "settled" label.

## 2. Findings (severity-ranked)

### C1 — Critical / defect — The ε-vs-stutter "different coordinates" identifiability claim collapses at period 1, and the design double-counts the slip *inside* the tract where HipSTR refuses to

> **RESOLVED 2026-06-22 (Option 1 — HipSTR's repeat-block rule).** `align` now admits
> **no sub-motif indel inside the tract**: in-tract length change is the whole-motif
> `Σ_Δ` slip only, `ε` is restricted to **substitutions** (gaps confined to flanks), so
> the identifiability claim is reworded **composition-vs-length** and holds at every
> period. Period-1 (mononucleotide) loci are **flagged**: their `ε` is substitutions-only
> and the stutter level absorbs the indistinguishable single-base slip; the
> homopolymer-length-as-gap upgrade is deferred. Applied to spec §4.2/§4.4/§6/§9,
> parameters §2, genotyping §4/§8/§9.

**Location:** spec §4.4 ("jointly identifiable because they move in different
coordinates (whole units vs bases)"), §6 ("`θ` only moves things by **whole motif units**
(`Δ`), `ε`/`align` only by **sub-motif, base-level** changes, so neither can absorb the
other's signal"); parameters §2 ("stutter moves whole motif units, `ε` moves bases").

**Claim challenged.** That ε (the per-base error inside `align`) and the stutter kernel
`S_θ` occupy disjoint coordinates and are therefore jointly identifiable off the confident
homozygotes.

**Why it's wrong.** For a **mononucleotide** locus (`period = 1` — poly-A/T, ubiquitous
and the most stutter-prone class in plant genomes) one repeat unit *is* one base. A −1
stutter (`Δ = −1`) and a single-base alignment deletion are the **same edit on the same
sequence**. The likelihood is `Qᵣ = Σ_Δ S_θ(Δ)·align(obs | cand ⊕ Δ)` and `align` is a
pair-HMM that *sums over small indels* under ε (spec §4.2 step 5: "sequencing + alignment
add … small indel"; §6: forward sums "over all the ways `obs` could arise"). So an
observed `A₉` from a true `A₁₀` homozygote receives mass from **two indistinguishable
paths**: `Δ = −1` exact-match (stutter), and `Δ = 0` with one HMM deletion (ε). The two
terms describe one physical event; the split between them is not identifiable, and the
`Σ_Δ` sum **double-counts** the contraction. During the pre-pass this means the −1 skirt
of a mono homozygote is attributed to stutter level while an unknown fraction of it is
polymerase/alignment indel — there is no second coordinate to separate them.

**The design is *more* exposed than the prior art it cites.** Verified in source: HipSTR
avoids exactly this by **spatial** separation — stutter applies *only inside the repeat
block* (`StutterAlignerClass`), the Dindel sequencing-indel model applies *only in the
flanks*, and `HapAligner.cpp:124-130` **explicitly blocks** sequencing indels from
extending into a stutter block ("preventing sequencing indels from extending into
preceding stutter blocks"). Within the homopolymer tract HipSTR has **no** per-base indel
term competing with stutter. At period 1 HipSTR's out-of-frame branch is dead code
(`stutter_model.cpp:34-37`, `bp_diff % 1 == 0` always) — the size change is *all* stutter,
by construction. This design instead runs a single flat-ε forward over the whole
repeat-region sequence (it dropped Stage-1's block/flank split, spec §2) **and** a stutter
sum on top — so ε-indels and stutter compete *inside the tract at every period*, which is
the confound HipSTR engineers away. The "different coordinates" sentence is not just wrong
at period 1; it is the opposite of how the cited tool achieves identifiability.

**Fix / forcing question.** Either (a) confine ε's indel component to flanks and make the
in-tract length change *purely* stutter (HipSTR's separation), or (b) for `period = 1`
collapse stutter and the in-tract indel into **one** term and acknowledge ε-substitution
is the only separately-identifiable base parameter there, or (c) exclude/flag period-1
loci from the ε/level pre-pass. At minimum, restate the identifiability claim with the
explicit carve-out "for `period ≥ 2`; at `period = 1` unit = base and the separation does
not hold." This also infects the simulator (A2): if the generative model emits the −1 mass
once but the scorer can explain it twice, "recover known parameters" (checkpoint D2) will
*look* fine while masking the non-identifiability.

### C2 — Critical / defect — Freezing the stutter *level* and *ε* re-opens the masquerading-het contamination that prior-review Issue 5 only neutralized for the (refinable) *shape*

> **RESOLVED 2026-06-22 (de-bias estimator + un-freeze the level).** Two changes: (i) the
> confident-homozygote hard gate is demoted to a **seed**; the final `ε`/level come from a
> **soft full-cohort EM responsibility reduce**, so population recurrence pulls a
> masquerading het's minor reads onto its *het* genotype instead of the skirt — removing the
> selection bias and killing the positive-feedback loop. (ii) The **stutter level is
> un-frozen** into the prior-side outer loop alongside `F` (it's an `S_θ` re-weight outside
> `align` → no cache rebuild, deterministic via the same fixed-order barrier reduce); **`ε`
> stays frozen** (in `align`; and post-C1 it is substitutions-only, *not* the contamination
> victim). Applied to spec §4.4 (C2 amendment + schedule + ingredient/§9), parameters
> §0/§1/§2/§4/§7/§9, genotyping §1/§4/§5/§7/§8/§9, roadmap D2/E1. Note the level/`F` reduces
> couple to **M1** (must be fixed-order).

**Location:** spec §4.4 amendment ("**both `ε` and the stutter level are frozen** at
sample-group granularity"); the schedule block lines 691-715 (per-locus EM "refine π AND
θ_locus shape", level/ε frozen); parameters §0/§1 (level + ε "Frozen per sample group").
Re-challenges prior review **Issue 5** ("resolved": *"θ demoted to a per-cell prior,
refined per-locus → seed contamination low-stakes"*).

**Claim challenged.** That confident-homozygote seed/estimate contamination by
masquerading hets is "low-stakes" because "the EM can overrule a biased prior."

**Why the resolution fails for the amended design.** Issue 5's resolution rests *entirely*
on the contaminated quantity being a **prior the per-locus EM refines** (`θ_locus` shrunk
toward `θ_period`). But the 2026-06-20/21 amendment introduced two **frozen** parameters
that the per-locus EM does **not** refine: the stutter **level** (`level_baseline +
level_slope·length`) and **ε**. The per-locus EM "refines only the locus *shape*
`θ_locus`" (spec line 520) — level and ε are fixed before genotyping and never overruled.
So the masquerading-het harm that Issue 5 identified (*"a 60/40 adjacent het … mislabelled
homozygote → its partner allele contaminates the estimate, inflating u/d"*) now lands on
parameters that are **permanent**, exactly the harm the "low-stakes" argument said was
gone. The resolution was written before the parameter it protects was frozen; it was never
re-examined against the freeze.

**And the co-evolving gate can *amplify* it (positive feedback).** The confident-homozygote
gate (one-vs-two-allele BIC, Q-P7) predicts the expected skirt from "the burn-in's current
params" (spec §4.4; parameters §2). If the current stutter level is **over**-estimated, a
true het's minor peak looks like *expected stutter* → the one-allele model wins → the het
is admitted as a homozygote → its partner allele is counted as stutter → the level is
pushed **higher** → still more hets masquerade. This is a runaway direction, not a
self-correcting one. The burn-in's "co-evolution" was introduced to fix the *opposite*
bias (high-stutter samples looking too clean, spec lines 560-569); it does not damp the
over-estimation→admit-more-hets loop, and there is no ground-truth anchor inside the loop
to stop it (see M4). The only brakes are the dev-computed defaults and simulator tuning —
i.e. the assumption that the loop starts inside the right basin.

**Why it matters.** A frozen, over-estimated level mis-calls hets **system-wide** in the
direction the spec itself names (§4.4 lines 480-482: high-stutter params applied to a
sample → "a genuine minor allele is absorbed as stutter → het **undercall**"). For a
precision-first popgen tool that is a structural diversity bias, not a borderline-call
flip.

**Fix / forcing question.** (i) State explicitly that the Issue-5 "low-stakes" argument
covers the *shape* only, and that **level and ε are not EM-overrulable**, so their seed
hygiene *is* load-bearing again. (ii) Carry a dominance/asymmetry gate (Mark-1's
`SEED_MIN_DOMINANCE_FRAC`, which Issue 5 noted was dropped) into level/ε estimation, not
just into θ⁰. (iii) Prove on the simulator that the burn-in loop is *contracting* (not
just convergent) under an injected high-stutter start, since the feedback direction above
is expansive. (iv) Consider estimating level *inside* the per-locus EM as a per-group
re-weight that the cohort can revise, rather than freezing it — the spec's own "re-weight,
no rebuild" argument (genotyping §4) says level is cheap to move, so the freeze buys
determinism, not cost, and could be relaxed.

### M1 — Major / defect — The byte-identity-across-threads guarantee is unproven for the one floating-point cross-locus reduce (`F_i`); "commutative reduce" does not buy it

> **RESOLVED 2026-06-22 (fixed-point integer accumulation).** The `F_i` and (post-C2)
> per-group level reduces sum per-(sample, locus) responsibilities whose loci complete out
> of order, so per-thread float partials would be thread-count-dependent. Both now use
> **fixed-point integer accumulation** (scale → round → sum into a per-individual /
> per-group `i128`; the same order-free `u64`-counts trick the pre-pass sufficient
> statistics already use), making the reduce associative + commutative → byte-identical
> regardless of thread count or completion order, with `O(N_samples)` memory and no
> buffering. The numerical/scientific impact of the old float wobble was independently
> judged negligible (~10⁻¹³, only ever a near-exact-tie call); the fix is taken because it
> keeps the project's byte-identity-across-threads invariant at ~zero cost. Applied to spec
> §4.4 (`F`/level outer loop + schedule), genotyping §5/§7/§8, parameters §7.

**Location:** genotyping §5 (the `F_i` reduce), §7 ("byte-identical across and within
thread counts"; "The `F_i` loop's `|ΔF| < tol` + max-rounds cap is a fixed,
order-independent stop"); parameters §3/§7 ("commutative reduce, order-independent"); spec
§4.4 lines 641-645.

**Claim challenged.** That Stage-2 calls are byte-identical across thread counts.

**Why it's risky.** The determinism argument conflates two different reduces:

- **The pre-pass sufficient statistics are integer** (`SlipProfile { down/up: [u64;…] }`,
  `SampleStutterStats { base_match/mismatch: u64, by_length: …u64 }`, parameters §3).
  Integer addition *is* associative and commutative → bit-exact regardless of thread
  count. **This part is genuinely deterministic** (credited in §3).
- **The `F_i` reduce is floating point.** `F_i` = mean autozygous-branch *responsibility*
  (a real number in `[0,1]`) summed over that individual's variable loci (genotyping §5,
  spec lines 657-664). Floating-point addition is **not** associative; the value depends on
  the summation tree. The docs say "per-thread partials reduced in a fixed (catalog)
  order" — but *per-thread partials* and *fixed catalog order* are in tension: a per-thread
  partial groups the addends by which loci that thread happened to own, which depends on
  the thread count. Adding the partials then yields a thread-count-dependent low-order-bit
  result. The "commutative reduce" language is correct for the integer accumulators and
  imported, unexamined, onto the float `F_i` reduce where it does not hold. The cited
  order-independence proof (the `|ΔF|` *stopping* test) is about *termination*, not about
  the *sum*.

`F_i` then flows through `shrink → clamp → clamp` and into the genotype prior; the reported
calls are the *final E-step under that `F_i`*. A last-bit difference in `F_i` can flip a
borderline genotype or GQ-gate, exactly the reproducibility loss the SNP path's determinism
invariant exists to prevent (and which the design repeatedly claims to carry forward).
Verified: `posterior_engine.rs` itself does **no** cross-locus float reduce — every record
is independent — so this reduce is *new* code outside the part whose determinism is
established.

**Fix / forcing question.** Specify the `F_i` reduce as a **fixed-order** accumulation
keyed by locus sequence number (the writer already reorders by locus seq —
reading §5), or accumulate in a fixed integer-scaled fixed-point representation, or use a
deterministic pairwise tree keyed by locus id. State which. Note the tension with bounded
streaming memory: a fixed-order sum needs the per-(individual, variable-locus)
contributions buffered or a deterministic tree — neither is free, and the
cohort-scaling/streaming design (reading §7) doesn't currently provide it. Until specified,
"byte-identical across thread counts" should be downgraded to "byte-identical *given a
fixed-order `F_i` reduce*, TBD."

### M2 — Major / defect — The per-sample align-cache story is internally contradictory and its worst case breaks the cohort-scaling memory thesis

> **RESOLVED 2026-06-22 (defer the cache).** The contradiction is removed by not building
> the optimization that caused it: the pre-materialized `align` table is an **unmeasured
> optimization v1 does not build** — v1 **recomputes `align` on demand** (post-C1 the
> in-tract score is a substitution closed-form + exact-match fast path, so the recompute is
> plausibly cheap; *measure first*). With no table there is no per-sample-vs-per-group `ε`
> keying decision, so the blow-up question is moot, and determinism is unaffected (`align`
> is a pure function of `(obs, cand⊕Δ, ε)` — identical bits whether recomputed or cached,
> no per-thread cache state). Sample grouping reverts to a pure estimation/reporting device.
> If measurement later shows `align` is the wall, the first step is a **per-locus memo** (no
> cross-sample `ε`-key → still no blow-up), and only then a persistent table bounded by
> per-group-`ε` quantization (not by shrinkage — that was the M2 flaw). Applied to spec
> §1/§4.4/§6/§9, genotyping §1/§4/§7/§8/§9 (Q-G3 rewritten, Q-G7 superseded), parameters
> §0/§1/§4/§9, roadmap B3/C2.

**Location:** genotyping §0 header + §4 + §7 ("`AlignCache` becomes **per-(locus, sample
group)** … ≤~8–10 versions"; struct `dims: (group × obs × cand × slip)`) **vs** genotyping
Q-G7 + parameters Q-P6 + spec §4.4 lines 515-518/533/1207-1209 ("the align cache **can be
per-sample** … its size is driven by the number of *distinct* `ε` values, which shrinkage
keeps small").

**Claim challenged.** That the align cache is bounded (≤~8–10 group slabs per locus) *and*
simultaneously "can be per-sample" because shrinkage keeps the number of distinct ε small —
so sample grouping is "an estimation + reporting device, not a cache constraint."

**Why it's wrong.** These two statements cannot both hold for the cohort the design
explicitly targets. The cache is keyed by **distinct ε values present at the locus**.
Shrinkage collapses *thin* samples onto neighbours' ε because shrinkage strength ∝
1/precision. But a **data-rich** sample has high precision → **negligible** shrinkage → it
keeps its **own** distinct ε. The design admits the (ε, level) cloud may be a **continuum,
not clusters** ("discrete-vs-continuum" is left as *"validation, not a gate"*, Q-G7 /
parameters §4 / spec §9). On a deep cohort with continuously-varying ε (precisely the
high-coverage non-model dataset this tool is for), the number of distinct ε approaches
**N**, and the per-locus align table becomes `O(N · obs · cand · slip)` — **the per-sample
cost the grouping existed to avoid**, and ~N/10× the "≤8–10" the genotyping doc budgets.
The memory bound therefore *depends* on the discrete-cluster hypothesis being true, while
the docs simultaneously declare that hypothesis "not a gate." That is the contradiction:
correctness of the headline memory property (the project's *memory-efficiency thesis*) is
gated on a question the design refuses to gate on.

**Fix / forcing question.** Pick one and make it load-bearing: (a) **quantize ε to a fixed
grid** (e.g. `K` bins), bounding the cache by construction — but then the cache *is* a
hard grouping and "can be per-sample / continuous" must be retracted; or (b) keep
continuous per-sample ε and **bound the cache differently** (e.g. cap distinct-ε slabs per
locus, evict, and accept recompute) and *measure* the worst case; or (c) keep the per-group
≤~8–10 claim and make grouping a genuine cache constraint, retracting Q-G7's
"per-sample." The `AlignCache` struct already hard-codes a `group` axis, so the code sketch
sides with (c) while the prose sides with (a/per-sample). Resolve the doc-vs-doc
disagreement, and add the "distinct ε ≈ N on a deep continuum" worst case to the §6/§7
memory benchmark that is currently "unmeasured."

### M3 — Major / reconsider — The load-bearing "stutter shape is cohort-shared" decision rests on human-PCR evidence the cited *code* does not encode, and the architecture cannot express a per-group shape if the invariance fails on the target data

> **RESOLVED 2026-06-22 (give the shape a sample-group axis).** The shape is no longer
> assumed cohort-shared: it is fit **per `(sample group, period)`, shrunk toward a
> cohort-per-period parent `θ_period`**, and the per-locus `θ_locus` refinement shrinks
> toward the sample's group-period shape. If the PCR-free "rate not profile" expectation
> holds, shrinkage collapses the groups to the shared shape at no cost; if it fails
> (non-model / degraded DNA / odd motifs), a data-rich group keeps its own profile — so
> invariance is now a **data-driven outcome with a built-in diagnostic** (the
> per-`(group,period)` spread vs the parent), not an architectural assumption. Cheap and
> determinism-neutral (shape enters only via the `S_θ` re-weight, never `align`; the skirt
> accumulator stays an integer order-free reduce, now keyed by `(group,period)` and fit
> *after* clustering). This is HipSTR's per-locus free-shape idea (`θ_locus`) **plus a
> group axis HipSTR lacks** (it pools all samples per locus). Applied to spec
> §3/§4.2/§4.4/§9, parameters §0/§1/§3/§4/§8/§9, genotyping §1/§4/§5/§9, roadmap A2/D2.

**Location:** parameters §0/§1 (shape "Cohort-shared … *Stable across protocol/preservation*
… PCR-free invariance"); spec §4.4 lines 488-505, §9 lines 1171-1190 ("protocol/preservation
move the stutter *rate*, not its *profile*"; Gymrek 2014, Willems NAR 2019, Chakraborty
1997).

**Claim challenged.** That stutter *shape* is protocol/preservation-invariant, so it can be
pooled cohort-wide (keyed by period) while only the *level* varies by sample group.

**Why it's risky.** (1) **Provenance.** Verified in source: neither HipSTR nor GangSTR's
code encodes "rate not profile." HipSTR re-estimates the *entire* shape per locus —
`in_geom_`, `in_up_`, `in_down_` are all free EM parameters
(`em_stutter_genotyper.cpp:117-126`); nothing is held as "shape" while a scalar "rate"
moves. Neither tool scales stutter rate with allele **length** at all (HipSTR's
`log_stutter_pmf` depends only on `bp_diff` and `motif_len_`, *not* `sample_bps`;
GangSTR's on `delta` only). So the design's central factorization — cohort-shared shape ×
per-group linear-in-length level — has **no precedent in the cited tools' code**; the
support is the *human-forensic/PCR-free papers* only, which I cannot independently verify
and which were measured on human PCR chemistry, not on wild-plant / degraded /
ancient-DNA / unusual-motif data this tool explicitly targets (spec §1, review prompt).
(2) **No recovery path if it fails.** The per-locus refinement (`θ_locus` shrunk to
`θ_period`) pools **across all groups present at the locus** — it is a *per-locus* axis, not
a *per-group* one. So a genuine per-**group** shape difference (e.g. ancient-DNA
deamination changing the up/down asymmetry, or a non-human polymerase with a different decay
ρ) is **structurally unrepresentable** in v1: it surfaces only as inflated per-locus
heterogeneity in the measure-step diagnostic and gets misattributed to "motif outliers."
The "granularity default = sample group unless data justify pooling" rule (parameters §0)
is stated, but shape is **hard-coded** to the cohort exception, and nothing in the
architecture can walk it back to per-group without a redesign.

**Fix / forcing question.** Make the invariance a **checked precondition**, not an
assumption: in the pre-pass, fit shape *per candidate group* on the richest groups and test
whether the per-group shapes differ beyond their error bars (the design already computes
per-sample stats — this is cheap). If they differ, warn and document that v1 cannot model
it. And soften the prior-art prose: cite it as "reported for human PCR-free libraries
(Gymrek 2014); **untested for non-model/degraded DNA**," not as settled prior art the code
inherits.

### M4 — Major / reconsider — "Calls don't move" is used as an acceptance criterion for parameters that *produce* the calls; the co-evolving burn-in can settle to a self-consistent wrong fixed point

> **RESOLVED 2026-06-22 (likelihood-based criteria + external correctness anchors).** Every
> "calls don't move" check is replaced by a quantity that references the *data fit*, not the
> model's agreement with itself (the C2 soft-EM made a single coherent objective available):
> (i) **burn-in stop = penalized marginal log-likelihood plateau** (`Δℓ_pen/|ℓ_pen| < tol`)
> — `ℓ_pen` is the E-step normalizer (≈free), EM-monotone, deterministic; a non-monotone
> `ℓ_pen` is a sharp bug/mismatch diagnostic. (ii) **ε-freeze and group-merge = penalized-
> likelihood / BIC model comparisons** (freeze/merge iff the richer/split model doesn't earn
> its parameters), not "does the output flip." (iii) The honest residual — a plateau means
> *converged*, not *correct* — is handled **outside** the loop by **multi-start** (best
> `ℓ_pen`, divergent basins flagged) plus the simulator ground-truth recovery / known-
> protocol positive control, now promoted from "validation" to the **correctness gates**.
> Applied to spec §4.4 (burn-in + measure + cluster + schedule + §9), parameters
> §4/Q-P2/Q-P4/Q-P5/Q-P6, review. (The old "(seed, batch) converges to the same fixed point"
> claim is dropped — `ℓ_pen` is evaluated on the full subset, so batch size affects speed,
> not the stop point.)

**Location:** parameters §4 / Q-P2 (ε-freeze: *"perturbing ε by ±1 SD flips no genotype
call"*), Q-P4 (*"(seed, batch size) … vary them, confirm calls don't move"*), Q-P6 /
spec §4.4 lines 542-544 (group merge *"only when it doesn't move calls"*); spec §4.4
lines 560-569 (selection/estimation co-evolve).

**Claim challenged.** That ε-freezing, group merging, and batch/seed choices are *licensed*
by checking that calls don't move under perturbation.

**Why it's risky.** Every one of these checks evaluates *the calls produced by the very
parameters being tuned*. "Calls don't move under a ±1 SD ε perturbation" establishes **local
insensitivity**, not **correctness** — a model can be robustly wrong. Worse, the
calls-don't-move test and the confident-homozygote gate share the *same* likelihood
machinery (Q-P1: one `rungs.rs`, one EM scoring), so the loci that "don't move" are loci the
model already finds self-consistent; the loci where the model is *biased* are
disproportionately the ones the gate excluded as "not confident homozygotes." The pre-pass
is thus a **bootstrap with no exogenous anchor**: selection depends on parameters, parameters
are estimated from the selected, and the acceptance test is self-referential. The design
*names* the selection-bias hazard (spec lines 560-569) and answers it with "co-evolve in the
burn-in loop" — but a co-evolving selection+estimation loop is exactly the structure that can
converge to a **stable but wrong** fixed point (cf. C2's positive-feedback direction). "Never
settling is a diagnostic" catches *divergence*; it gives **false assurance on convergence** —
a loop that settles cleanly to the wrong basin reports success.

**Fix / forcing question.** Demote "calls don't move" from *license* to *necessary-but-not-
sufficient*, and make the **simulator ground-truth recovery** (checkpoint D2) and the
**known-protocol positive control** (currently "validation, not gates", spec §9) the actual
gates — the only checks with an anchor outside the loop. Add an adversarial simulator case:
inject a high-stutter group and a masked-het-heavy locus set, start the burn-in from
*wrong* defaults, and show it recovers truth rather than a self-consistent wrong fixed
point. Until that exists, the ε-freeze "checked invariant" (Q-P2) is checking the wrong
thing.

### M5 — Major / defect — Null-allele contamination spreads through `F_i` shrinkage to the cohort mean, contradicting the "per-individual `F_i` localizes a bad sample" claim

> **RESOLVED 2026-06-22 (split the finding; null premise downgraded, Wahlund made a user
> warning).** The null-allele half rests on a premise that doesn't transfer: classical null
> alleles arise from **primer-binding-site mutations** (a PCR-fragment / capillary
> phenomenon) — this caller is **primer-free sequencing**, so the dominant mechanism largely
> doesn't apply (residual = reference/mapping bias; capture/RAD can reintroduce some). The
> empirical null-allele literature (Brookfield 1996; van Oosterhout 2004 / MICRO-CHECKER;
> Chapuis & Estoup 2007) is all capillary-microsatellite; no strong evidence nulls are a
> practical `F_IS` problem in sequencing-based STR calling. So the per-locus null mechanism
> stays **deferred, and that's appropriate for WGS** (capture/RAD may revisit). The half
> that **survives is general**: any cohort-wide hom-excess — chiefly **Wahlund/population
> structure** — inflates the cohort mean and is broadcast by shrinkage; but Wahlund is *real
> structure*, hard to remove cleanly, so v1 **reports apparent `F_IS` with an explicit user
> warning rather than correcting it**, and the "localizes a bad sample" claim is narrowed to
> an *idiosyncratic* outlier (which shrinkage does contain) vs. a cohort-wide signal (which
> is real and reported, not localized away). The warning is made an **output obligation**
> (`vcf_out.rs`), not just rationale. Applied to spec §4.2/§4.4/§4.5/§9, genotyping §5/§6.

**Location:** spec §4.4 lines 666-689 ("shrink it toward the cohort-mean `F`"; "a
contaminated or null-heavy sample inflates only its own `F_i`, not the whole cohort's");
caveat lines 684-689 ("apparent `F_IS`", "self-reinforcing loop"); genotyping §5; §9
lines 1231-1238 (null alleles deferred).

**Claim challenged.** That per-individual `F_i` "localizes a bad sample's effect" and that
null-allele bias is a deferrable refinement.

**Why it's wrong.** Two mechanisms the design states *individually* combine to **delocalize**
the bias it claims to localize:

1. Null alleles are typically a **locus** property (a primer/mapping dropout common across
   the cohort), not a single-sample one. A locus with a common null produces apparent
   homozygote excess in **every carrier**, raising **many** `F_i` simultaneously → the
   **cohort mean `F` is inflated**.
2. The design then **shrinks every individual's `F_i` toward that cohort mean** (spec
   line 667, "hierarchical … sparsely-typed individuals shrink harder"). Shrinkage toward an
   inflated mean **pulls clean, well-typed samples' `F_i` up too**, suppressing their
   genuine hets (the `F·π` prior down-weights heterozygous genotypes).

So the inflation does **not** stay local — shrinkage is the exact channel that broadcasts it.
The "localizes a bad sample's effect" claim (line 672) is true only for an *idiosyncratic*
contaminated sample whose `F_i` is an outlier the shrinkage pulls *down*; it is **false** for
the common case (a null-prone locus across the cohort), where shrinkage pulls everyone *up*.
The two sentences (lines 667 and 672) describe shrinkage working in opposite directions and
cannot both be load-bearing.

**Fix / forcing question.** (i) Acknowledge that with cohort-wide null-prone loci the
deferred per-locus null mechanism (spec lines 1231-1238) is **load-bearing, not optional** —
it is what stops null excess from entering the cohort mean. (ii) Until built, exclude
high-hom-excess loci that are inconsistent with the cohort `F` from the `F_i` reduce
(Micro-Checker-style), which the spec already sketches as the deferred fix; promote it to v1
for the `F` *reduce input* even if the per-locus null-frequency model stays deferred. (iii)
Reconcile lines 667 and 672 explicitly.

### m1 — Minor/Medium / reconsider — `G₀` mode-centring trades reference bias for a *unimodality* bias, in exactly the structured cohorts the tool targets

**Location:** spec §4.3 lines 397-415, §9 lines 1157-1164; parameters §5. Re-challenges
prior-review **Issue 8** ("resolved" by centring `G₀` on the per-locus cohort modal allele).

**Why it's risky.** Issue 8 correctly killed *reference*-centring. But centring the
geometric pseudocounts on the **cohort modal allele** assumes the allele-size distribution
is **unimodal** around one mode (the spec says so: "SMM-consistent … unimodal around *its
own* mode", line 408). Wild tomato with population structure / Wahlund (a stated target, and
the very thing the `F_IS` discussion invokes) is frequently **bi/multimodal**: two common
alleles far apart. The mode is then just one of them, and the *other* common allele sits far
out on the geometric tail with a `p^|Δ|` floor — re-creating, for the second mode, precisely
the "true modal allele gets a floor so small the EM can't grow it" failure Issue 8 raised
against the reference (line 406). The masked-het-recovery argument (the whole reason `G₀`
exists) is weakest exactly where carriers of the distant second allele need it. The spec
hand-waves this ("multimodal cohorts left to the data, not the prior", line 1164) but the
prior is *actively* mode-peaked and re-enters **every** M-step, so it fights the data at
small N rather than staying neutral.

**Fix / forcing question.** For the regularizer role, consider a **flatter / heavier-tailed
or mixture** base measure when the candidate pool shows multimodality (cheap to detect from
the assembly pool already in hand), or cap the decay so a distant real mode is not floored.
At minimum, state the unimodality assumption and that structured/multimodal cohorts (a
target) violate it, mirroring the honesty Issue 8 demanded of reference-centring.

**Why it matters.** (a) **Zero confident homozygotes in the whole cohort** (a highly

simulator rather than asserting it.

## 3. Things I tried to break but couldn't

- **Pre-pass commutative-reduce determinism (integer stats).** `SlipProfile` and
  `SampleStutterStats` are `u64` counts; integer addition is order-independent, so the
  per-period shape and per-sample level/ε *are* bit-identical across thread counts.
  The determinism claim holds **here** — only the float `F_i` reduce (M1) is exposed.
- **Per-locus EM determinism.** Each locus EM is a pure function of (locus data, frozen
  globals, `F_i` vector); given a fixed `F_i` it is genuinely reproducible. Confirmed the
  reused engine does no cross-record float accumulation (`posterior_engine.rs`, "independent
  per-record EM loop").
- **Slip-placement marginalization provenance.** The "marginalize *which copy* the slip lands
  on inside `align`" claim is a faithful HipSTR borrow — `StutterAlignerClass` sums (not
  max) over sequence-aware placements with a uniform position prior
  (`StutterAlignerClass.cpp:63-150`). Genuinely HipSTR, correctly credited.
- **Absorbing-trap guards.** The `π=0` geometric pseudocount floor and the `F_CEILING=0.99`
  ceiling are sound and correctly mirror each other; the `F` reduce restricted to variable
  loci is the right information set.
- **Cohort-recurrence vs within-sample jackpotting.** Rung recurrence counts *samples*, so
  within-sample PCR duplication cannot manufacture a recurrent false allele at the cohort
  level (the within-sample exposure is m2's residual).
- **Per-sample `F` engine input.** Verified: the engine consumes a per-sample fixation-index
  vector as input to the IBD prior (`fixation_index_overrides` → `log_f_per_sample`), so the
  per-individual `F_i` loop needs no prior-side engine change — the design's narrow reuse
  claim is correct (it only over-claims when it implies the engine *estimates* `F`, which it
  does not — prior-review Issue 3, still correctly closed).
- **Pseudocount-vector generalization feasibility.** The engine already materializes
  pseudocounts as an opaque per-allele `Vec<f64>` before the M-step; swapping the
  class-routed fill for a supplied per-candidate `G₀` vector is genuinely low-invasiveness on
  the math path (caveat: the contamination/compound class-coupling must be bypassed, which
  the design already says it bypasses).
